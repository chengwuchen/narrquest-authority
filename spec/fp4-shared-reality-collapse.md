# FP-4: Shared Reality Collapse (Draft)

Version: 0.1.0-draft  
Status: Experimental (non-canonical)  
Derived from: FBA-2026-03-tone-boundary-v2

---

## 1. Definition

Shared Reality Collapse refers to a failure mode in which multiple
internally coherent but mutually incompatible narratives coexist,
and no mechanism remains to establish a jointly verifiable epistemic frame.

In this condition, "reality" is no longer discovered or reconstructed,
but temporarily stabilized by authority, alignment, or selection.

---

## 2. Core Properties

- Non-recoverability of truth via provenance
- Absence of shared adjudication framework
- Coexistence of incompatible but plausible narratives
- Collapse of common epistemic anchor

---

## 3. Boundary Conditions

### Distinction from FP-1 (Provenance Collapse)

FP-1 assumes:
- truth exists but source is uncertain
- verification can restore confidence

FP-4 assumes:
- no shared condition exists to confirm truth
- provenance cannot resolve disagreement

---

### Distinction from FP-3 (Interpretive Overreach)

FP-3 assumes:
- excessive abstraction distorts interpretation

FP-4 assumes:
- even minimal interpretation cannot reconcile narratives
- conflict is structural, not interpretive

---

## 4. Trigger Conditions

- High-volume synthetic media (e.g. deepfake saturation)
- Conflicting authority claims without resolution
- Verification latency exceeds narrative spread
- Information environment lacks trusted anchor

---

## 5. Observable Signals

- classification_check shows "unresolved"
- multi-pattern overlap without convergence
- drift escalates into non-classifiability
- narrative authority replaces evidentiary validation

## 7. Status

FP-4 is currently a provisional extension.

It is supported by cross-event boundary analysis but is not yet integrated
into the canonical failure taxonomy (failure-spec.md).

Further validation required across additional event groups.
